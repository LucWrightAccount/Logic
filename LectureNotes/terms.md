# Terms

**Propositional Logic**  

*Definition*: The branch of Logic that studies ways of combining or altering statements(propositions) to form more complex statements, evaluating their truth values(true or false) based on connectives.



**Logic Symbols**- Negation($\neg$)
- Conjunction($\wedge$)
- Disjunction($\vee$)
- Implication($\to$)
- Biconditional($\leftrightarrow$)

**Predicate Logic**

*Definintion:* a formal system that models relationships and properties fo objects, break dowm statements into variables, predicates, and quantifiers

*Example:* "for all x is a human, then x is mortal"
($\forall x(H(x)\rightarrow M(x))$)
to define logical rules and infrence.

**Predicates**

*Defintion:* Represents attributes of objects or relation ships between them. 

*Example:* "Socrates is a Human" $(H(s)) ,H$ is a predicate

**Variables and Constants**

*Definition:* Variables $(x,y)$ act as place holders, while constants $(a, b, s)$ name specific individuals

**Quantifiers**

*Defination:* Defines the range of variables
- Universial($\forall x$) : For all x in the universe
- Existential ($\exists x$): Theres at least one x
